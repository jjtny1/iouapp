IOU — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 17 May 2026

IOU ("IOU", "we", "us") is a service that helps a group split a restaurant bill: a host uploads a receipt photo, friends claim the items they ordered, and each person settles their share. This policy explains what information IOU collects, how it is used, and the third parties involved. It applies to the IOU website at iouapp.ai and the IOU iOS app.

Information we collect

  • Email address. Sign-in is passwordless. When you sign in, you give us your email address and we email you a one-time "magic link" to log in. We keep your email address to identify your account.
  • Session token. After you sign in, a session cookie or token is stored on your device so you stay logged in. It is not used for advertising or cross-site tracking.
  • Receipt photos. If you host a bill, you may upload a photo of a restaurant receipt so the app can read its line items, tax, tip, and total.
  • Audio (optional "auto-split"). If you use the optional auto-split feature by voice, you may record or upload a short audio clip describing who ordered what; it is transcribed to text. You can instead type the split as text, in which case no audio is collected.
  • Bill and split data. We store the bills you create, their line items, claims, currency, tax/tip/service amounts, and the display names of participants in a bill.
  • Venmo handle. Hosts may save a Venmo handle so friends can pay them. Payment itself is a hand-off to the Venmo app — IOU does not process payments and never sees your Venmo password or financial account credentials.

How we use your information

We use the information above solely to provide the service: to sign you in, to parse receipts into items, to transcribe an auto-split description, to let friends claim items and see what they owe, and to hand off payment to Venmo. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for advertising.

Third parties

To provide the service, IOU shares limited data with the following service providers:

  • Anthropic. Uploaded receipt photos are sent to Anthropic's API to extract line items and totals from the image.
  • OpenAI. If you use the voice auto-split feature, the audio clip is sent to OpenAI's Whisper API to transcribe it to text.
  • Amazon Web Services (AWS). IOU's server and database are hosted on AWS infrastructure.
  • Amazon SES. The sign-in "magic link" email is delivered through Amazon Simple Email Service.
  • Venmo. When a friend chooses to pay, IOU opens Venmo with a pre-filled amount and note. The payment happens entirely within Venmo under Venmo's own terms and privacy policy.

These providers process data on our behalf to deliver their part of the service. We encourage you to review their respective privacy policies.

Data storage and security

Bill data, account information, and related records are stored in a database on IOU's server, hosted on AWS. We take reasonable measures to protect this data, but no method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

Data retention and deletion

We keep your data for as long as your account is active and you continue to use the service. You can delete a bill ("tab") you host at any time from within the app, which removes its items and claims. If you would like your account and associated personal information deleted, contact us at the address below and we will process your request.

Children's privacy

IOU is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the effective date at the top of this page.

Contact

For privacy questions or data deletion requests, contact us at privacy@iouapp.ai.
